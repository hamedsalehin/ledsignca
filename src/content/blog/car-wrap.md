## Car wrap

### Foiling and car wrap: how does car wrap work?

Wrapping a vehicle is done in four steps. In principle, almost all vehicles or even individual components and countless interior components can be wrapped .

*   Step: Before foiling, the vehicle must be washed, whereby polish or wax should be avoided so that the foil adheres well.
*   Step: The car is cleaned again by the foiled so that the foil does not come off later. This professional cleaning takes about 2 hours. Then the smaller body parts are dismantled, such as taillights, door handles, bumpers and if possible other components such as the doors, hood or tailgate. In this way, the foil can be glued more precisely and areas that are difficult to access, such as the entrances, can be foiled without any problems.
*   Step: The vehicle is measured and the wrap is roughly cut to size. First, details such as the recesses for fog lights or the air intakes are processed. The wrap is then placed on the surface to be covered and then smoothed over the vehicle using a squeegee. It is important that bubble formation is avoided. Meanwhile, the foil is heated to approx. 70 to 85 degrees with the hot air gun. This makes it more elastic and so it can be optimally adapted to the car.
*   Step 4: Protruding wrap remnants are then cut off precisely with a cutter knife. Then you lay them over the edges and work them into small cracks. Finally, the wrap is sealed and the vehicle is polished to a high gloss using a special product. Attention: The vehicle that has just been wrapped in foil must not be cleaned in the car wash for 2 to 3 weeks, as the foil can become detached again.

## Car wrap

### Can every car be wrapped ?

Every vehicle can be wrapped. Almost anything is possible with this procedure, from full car wrap to partial wrapping. However, it is important for a vehicle with a rare or old paint (old-timer) to note that the foiling is first carried out on an inconspicuous place for testing. Undesirable reactions of the paint with the adhesive of the wrap can thus be prevented . Therefore, you can also test the removal of the foil and check whether the paint can withstand the procedure at all. In addition, if you want, you can wrap the interior parts of a car, such as the center console or parts of the dashboard (trims, etc.). Even small changes to the vehicle can be implemented for a small amount! Attention: Some interior materials cannot be wrapped well. A coated plastic is one such example. It is possible that the foil simply does not stick to it. Ask a professional if the wrapping can be done inside the car.

## Is it possible to wrap a car yourself?

The employees of the special workshops know how to handle the car foil very well. This ensures that air pockets and errors due to excessive dust exposure can be prevented. A room in which a car is covered with foil should also be 16 - 25 °C warm. You should also trust experienced, well-trained wrappers when removing the foil. Be sure to avoid the following mistakes: Do not let the wrap get too hot, as this can result in unsightly shiny or faded areas. Always cut out the foil generously. Remove body parts such as exterior mirrors, door handles, etc. that interfere with foiling. Test the adhesion of the wrap in advance! Only use suitable tools such as a squeegee, knifeless tape, paper tape, etc. Choose a good quality wrap as it will last longer and be easier to work with.
